Detailed Description

Radiation parameters for synchrotron and inverse Compton emission processes.

This class encapsulates the microphysical parameters that govern particle acceleration and radiation processes in relativistic shocks. It controls the efficiency of converting shock energy into accelerated electrons and magnetic fields, as well as enabling advanced radiation processes.

Construct radiation model with given microphysical parameters.

Initializes the radiation physics model with parameters controlling particle acceleration efficiency, magnetic field generation, and advanced radiation processes. These parameters are fundamental to afterglow modeling as they determine the spectral shape and overall normalization of the emission.

Parameters
eps_eFraction of shock energy transferred to relativistic electrons
eps_BFraction of shock energy stored in magnetic field
pElectron energy spectral index (typically 2.2-2.8)
xi_eFraction of shock-heated electrons that are accelerated to relativistic energies
ssc_coolingWhether to include inverse Compton cooling of electrons
sscWhether to include synchrotron self-Compton emission (default: false)
knWhether to include Klein-Nishina corrections for IC processes (default: false)
