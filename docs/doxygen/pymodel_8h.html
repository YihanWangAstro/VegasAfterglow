Creates a Gaussian jet model with smooth energy and Lorentz factor profiles.

Generates an ejecta structure representing a Gaussian-structured jet where both the energy density and initial Lorentz factor decrease smoothly from the jet axis following a Gaussian profile. This model provides a more realistic smooth transition compared to the sharp cutoff of top-hat jets and is often used to model structured jets in gamma-ray bursts.

Parameters

Gamma0Initial bulk Lorentz factor at the jet center

durationDuration of central engine activity [seconds] (default: 1)
magnetarOptional magnetar model for continuous energy injection (default: none)
Returns

Creates a top-hat jet model with uniform energy and Lorentz factor distributions.

Generates an ejecta structure representing a top-hat jet where the energy density and initial Lorentz factor are constant within the core angle theta_c and zero outside. Creates a stellar wind environment with r^(-2) density profile.

Generates a medium structure representing a stellar wind from a massive star progenitor, with density following ρ(r) = A* /(4πr²) where A_* is the wind parameter. This model is appropriate for gamma-ray bursts occurring in the vicinity of massive stars such as Wolf-Rayet stars or other evolved massive stars with strong stellar winds.
